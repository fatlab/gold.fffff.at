Lindsay Howard

Curator
website

Lindsay Howard is a curator and researcher based in New York. She is the Curatorial Director of 319 Scholes, a not-for-profit exhibition space in Brooklyn dedicated to promoting works at the intersection of art and technology.She is the Curatorial Fellow at Eyebeam and curator of F.A.T 5 GOLD.

DJ RIPLEY

DJ, Scholar, Legal Etnographer
website

Dr. Larisa Mann (PhD, UC Berkeley Law School) mixes music to highlight difference rather than seamlessness and to reveal the physical and legal borders where music experiences occur, often, outside legal frameworks of ownership and licenses. She will join F.A.T to moderate the panel "Rights, Rogues and Refugees".

Joe Karaganis

Vicepresident of The American Assembly at Columbia University
website

His work focuses on the relationship between digital convergence and cultural production, and has recently included research on broadband adoption, data policy, and media piracy. Editor of the groundbreaking study "Media Piracy on Emerging Economies" which he will discuss in the panel "Rights, Rogues and Refugees".

DJ /RUPTURE

DJ,artist,writer
website

Jace Clayton practice explores how sound, technology use in low-income communities, and public space interact, with an emphasis on Latin America, Africa, and the Arab world. He hosts Mudd Up in WFMU, runs a book club and network communities beyond the digital. He will join F.A.T in the panel "Rights, Rogues and Refugees".

KEVIN DRISCOLL

website

Kevin Driscoll is a PhD candidate in the Annenberg School for Communication and Journalism at University of Southern California. His recent research concerns popular technical cultures in the U.S.including hobbyist telecommunication networks and the regional economics of early personal computing. Previously, he earned an MS in comparative media studies from MIT and taught computer science at Prospect Hill Academy Charter School.
